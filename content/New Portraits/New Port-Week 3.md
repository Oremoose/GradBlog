# Reading:
### The Work of Art in the Age of Mechanical Reproduction by Walter Benjamin

I disagree with the author. The whole essay was giving "old man yells at cloud " vibes, and the Social media post that "show how bad modern art is" while showing footage of contemporary artists.  Like at one point he brings up "The cult of the movie actor" as a critique of cinema as if stage actors don't also have cult following pre-movie world.

I felt like the author begging the question the entire time making assumptions or stating opinions as facts just to prove his own points.  The obsession of the "aura" , which the author crudely defined the authenticity and authority of a piece, something that can't actually be measured and, as I would argue, does not matters in art. 
For instance when trying to compare cinema and painting. He complains that films are void of "aura". Planting his flag on an idea like we should meditate on the impact of each frame of a movie like there are 24 frames of masterpieces flickering in front our faces each second. ANd does not even acknowledge that the Movie as a whole is the art piece and that we should reflect on it in its entirety.  

I ultimately felt like there was the notion that art is made separated from the artist, and that the art is made despite of the artist. It reminded me too much of the "Anti-mondern art" posts on line where they take art out of context and then, without taking the time to really thing the piece through, calling it bad because its not like the art that was made be dead white men in the Museums. The ability to replicate art makes art more accessible and therefore more impactful. The best art is not the pieces that cost a lot of money, nor what is hard to access rather its the ones that move the most people and that can't be done if the piece is distributed widely.
