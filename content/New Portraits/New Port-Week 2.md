I also read [In Defense of the Poor Image.](https://www.e-flux.com/journal/10/61362/in-defense-of-the-poor-image)

My thoughts on it is that I agree with Hito the author.
I feel like it fits in with my own thought that the is not "Bad" art. The art that is made might not be for you, even if you make it yourself. The poor image is not seen a "valuable" because it is not clear crisp and clean. But the best ideas are mostly half baked and messy so the fact that we disregard what could be an impactful image because its been compressed to high heaven is real interesting. 

I also find it interesting how those who pirate and archive what could be lost media are typically seen in dark light because their actions can hurt the bottom dollar of ultra rich mega corps. Dispite they are saving the real bed rock of culture. I find that the most popular and impactful artists are usually formed and are influenced by the niche and unknowns that are known and only can be found where the "poor images" live.
