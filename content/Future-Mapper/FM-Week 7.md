#### 2. Whitney Biennial Observation

### Basel Abbas & Ruanne Abou-Rahme
![[IMG_4222.png]]

![[IMG_4219.png]]



- This Multiwalled projection work was powerful and engulfed my sense. After sitting in the space for ten or so minutes I had to leave because I found myself becoming emotional.
- This was the only work that really moved me internally and made me feel like the art was more than art.
- "Ruanne Abou-Rahme and Basel Abbas express what they have described as collective feelings of love and longing felt by many Palestinians in the wake of erasure of land and communities in Palestine." - [Whitney site](https://whitney.org/exhibitions/2026-biennial/art?section=1) This explains quite well how the piece is related to the theme of "relationality". I felt great privilege to be able to have a small taste of what it feels like to have so much taken away from your community and still have the courage to persist.
- Further more I feel like it is very simple to see why they were added to the Biennial. With the Genocide that is persisting in Palestine their work speaks volumes of the struggle and hurt as well as the beauty of the Palestinian people.
